The Unexpected History of the Hot Dog

作者: Funday • 日期: 2024/11/05

When you bite into a hot dog at a baseball game, you probably aren't thinking about history. But the humble hot dog has a surprisingly rich and complex past that stretches back centuries.

While often associated with the US, the hot dog's origins are European, specifically Germany. The frankfurter sausage, a predecessor, dates back to the 15th century. It became popular in Frankfurt, giving it its name.

It wasn't until the 19th century that German immigrants brought these sausages to America. Street vendors quickly adopted them. The term 'dachshund sausage' was an early name used in the US, referring to its long, thin shape.

The name 'hot dog' itself is debated. A popular, though likely false, legend credits a cartoonist in 1901 who couldn't spell 'dachshund' and simply wrote 'hot dog!' instead. Regardless of the true origin, the name stuck, and the food became an American staple.

當你在棒球比賽中咬下一口熱狗時，你可能不會想到歷史。但這不起眼的熱狗卻有著令人驚訝的豐富而複雜的歷史，可以追溯到幾個世紀前。

雖然熱狗通常與美國聯繫在一起，但它的起源卻在歐洲，特別是德國。法蘭克福香腸是它的前身，可以追溯到 15 世紀。它在法蘭克福流行起來，因此得名。

直到 19 世紀，德國移民才將這些香腸帶到美國。街頭小販很快就採用了它們。 「臘腸香腸」（dachshund sausage）是美國早期使用的名稱，指的是它又長又細的形狀。

「熱狗」（hot dog）這個名字本身仍有爭議。一個流行的、儘管可能是錯誤的傳說，歸功於一位漫畫家在 1901 年因為拼不出「臘腸犬」（dachshund）這個詞，而直接寫下「熱狗！」（hot dog!）。無論真正的起源如何，這個名字都沿用了下來，這種食物也成為美國的主食。
